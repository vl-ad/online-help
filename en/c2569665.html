Widgets

Widgets are predefined groupings to help you quickly access files with just one click of the mouse. A few examples are listed below:

  • Recently Opened - This widget displays items that you’ve interacted with recently. Useful when you’re working with the same set of items day in and day out.
  • Duplicates - This widget lists duplicate files. Use this widget to find out how many copies of a file you may have and where the file is located.
  • Large Files - This widget keeps a running list of the largest files on your sources. It’s a great way to find out what in the world is taking up so much space! From there you can delete or move the large items to make room where you need it most.
  • This week in history - This widget travels back into the past to resurface files from every year ago this week. Check here every few days and you’re bound to see something new!
  • This month in history - This widget travels back into the past to resurface files from every year ago this month. Check at the end of each month and you can finally say, once and for all, if this year is shaping up to be better than the last.
  • Celebrate - This widget compiles your photos that are tagged with cake, candle, and/or balloon. It’s a good way to find recently cataloged content after you’ve had a party, or just relive those moments when you’re feeling nostalgic.
